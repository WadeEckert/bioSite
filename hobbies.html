Hobbies & Interests

Cooking for Family

Outside of her professional life, Leigh's creativity shows up in more personal ways, especially in the kitchen. Cooking has become one of the ways she takes care of the people around her, whether it's preparing everyday meals or cooking for family during the holidays.

She has a natural instinct for it, often relying on smell and experience rather than strict measurements. From slow-cooked roasts to homemade bread and traditional Hispanic dishes, cooking is both a creative outlet and a way she brings people together.

Mountain Drives

Some of Leigh's favorite moments are the simplest ones, like driving through the mountains and visiting small towns. These trips give her a chance to slow down and step away from the structure of daily life.

Surrounded by nature, she's able to reset, reflect, and enjoy the quiet in a way that contrasts with her busy work and creative projects. It's a reminder of how important balance is in her life.

Art Museums

Even as her career has moved into the digital world, Leigh has never lost her connection to traditional art. Visiting museums is something she continues to enjoy, not just for inspiration but for appreciation.

Seeing the work of other artists, across different styles and time periods, keeps her connected to the creative world that first inspired her. It also influences how she approaches her own work, reminding her that creativity is always evolving.

Writing Stories

One of the most personal parts of Leigh's creative life is her writing. Since high school, she has been developing stories that she is now actively working to bring to life.

Her projects include a three-part Weird West series with dark, psychological, and paranormal elements, as well as a medieval fantasy with a mystery aspect. While these stories are still in progress, they represent a long-term goal she has carried with her for years, and a commitment to telling stories in her own voice.

“Creativity is not limited to one place. It shows up in work, stories, food, and everyday life.”
