About Leigh

Creative. Technical. Resilient.

Leigh Barerra is a front-end developer and graphic designer whose work blends creativity with technical problem-solving. Born on March 9, 1990, in Dallas, Texas, she grew up in a family that valued both intellectual rigor and professional discipline, as her parents operated a law office together. Despite this environment, Leigh developed an early passion for art, which became a defining influence throughout her life.

During her high school years, Leigh relocated with her parents to Las Vegas, New Mexico, where she graduated in 2008. She initially pursued her artistic interests through formal education, attending art school in both Boston and Portland. However, her academic path was interrupted by a prolonged and challenging medical condition. After years of uncertainty and misdiagnosis, she was diagnosed with degenerative bone disease in her hip, which ultimately required a full hip replacement. This experience significantly shaped her perspective, reinforcing her resilience and prompting her to reevaluate her career direction.

After relocating to Denver, Colorado, Leigh began rebuilding her path by exploring both teaching and technology. She worked as a substitute teacher and also taught coding to younger students online, which helped spark a deeper interest in digital development. She later completed her degree in Web Design and Graphic Design at Bellevue University, where she developed a strong foundation in both visual design and front-end development.

Leigh currently works at RTI (Research Triangle Institute), where she applies her skills to create engaging and functional digital experiences. Beyond her professional work, she is an active writer, developing multiple works of fiction, including a three-part Weird West series and a medieval epic fantasy. Her writing often explores psychological depth, alternative history, and immersive world-building.

In her personal life, Leigh enjoys spending time with her husband, Wade, as well as their two dogs, exploring art museums, cooking, and traveling to small mountain towns. She is driven by a desire to continue growing both creatively and technically, with goals of expanding her back-end programming skills and completing her long-developed literary projects.

“I like understanding things deeply, whether that's ideas, people, or systems.”
